# Short-Form Creative Rubric

Scores use a `0-3` scale.

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 0 | Missing, unclear, or actively harmful. |
| 1 | Present but weak, delayed, generic, or hard to understand. |
| 2 | Functional and clear, with some room for sharper execution. |
| 3 | Strong, specific, memorable, and supported by visible evidence. |

## Dimensions

| Dimension | Score evidence required |
|---|---|
| Hook strength | First-frame read, opening line, visible problem, surprise, proof, or pattern interrupt. |
| Promise clarity | Timestamp where the payoff becomes understandable. |
| Emotional arc | Evidence of movement from pain/desire to relief/control/status. |
| Brand/product integration | First product/brand appearance and whether it supports the story. |
| Visual memorability | Distinct visual motif, face, motion, UI moment, transformation, or contrast. |
| Message hierarchy | Alignment between captions, voiceover, on-screen text, and visuals. |
| Pacing/edit rhythm | Cut cadence, dead seconds, repeated shots, comprehension load. |
| Core story/idea | The central transformation, reveal, demo, contradiction, or proof mechanism. |
| CTA strength | Specificity, timing, and connection to the promise. |
| Likely drop-off risk | Timestamped moments where attention or comprehension may break. |
| Proof credibility | Demo, result, comparison, social proof, or believable mechanism. |
| Caption readability | Size, contrast, safe zones, sync, and mobile legibility. |
| Audience fit | Fit for creators/agencies and their editing/output-volume pain. |
| Copycat/IP risk | Whether adaptation risks copying protected expression instead of mechanism. |
| Originality and relevance | Whether the idea is both distinctive and strategically appropriate. |
| Human truth | Whether the creative understands a specific private frustration, desire, fear, or status tension. |
| Swappability | Whether the ad still works if Submagic is replaced by another AI editor; lower swappability is stronger. |
| Creative intent clarity | Whether the likely hypothesis, audience, funnel stage, KPI, and offer strategy can be inferred from the evidence without pretending performance data exists. |

## Required Analysis Layers

Each scored dimension should be supported by:

- observation: what is visible or audible
- viewer perception: what the viewer likely understands or feels
- mechanism: what persuasion structure is operating
- human truth: the private frustration, fear, desire, status tension, or operational truth underneath the surface beat
- belief shift: what the viewer believed before the beat and what the creative asks them to believe after it
- transfer principle: what can be reused for Submagic without copying competitor expression
- evidence ID: the artifact-backed source of the claim
- creative intent: the campaign hypothesis, audience, funnel stage, KPI, and offer strategy the visible execution implies

## Creative Intent Gate

Every deconstruction must explain why the creative was likely made before explaining how it works.

A useful `creative_intent` read identifies:

- the likely advertiser hypothesis
- the target audience and awareness stage
- the funnel stage and likely KPI
- the offer strategy that connects hook to action
- the marketer job-to-be-done
- the missing data that would change the inference

Reject reports that imply access to targeting, spend, ROAS, or conversion data when only the video creative is available.

## Core-Understanding Gate

Reject deconstructions that only summarize the timeline. A useful report must identify:

- the deepest viewer tension the creative exploits
- the proof ladder that makes the promise feel concrete
- the skeptical read that could break persuasion
- the counterfactual change most likely to improve comprehension or retention
- the Submagic-native transfer principle, phrased as a workflow or creator-output mechanism rather than a copied visual surface

## Core-Understanding Quality Gate

A high-quality deconstruction must identify:

- The human truth the creative exploits.
- The belief shift it tries to create.
- The mechanism that moves attention into persuasion.
- The proof debt that may cause skepticism.
- The transferable principle that can become a Submagic-native creative.

Reject shallow reports that only describe what appears on screen or assign scores without explaining viewer psychology.

## Distilled Report Gate

The visible HTML report depends on `distilled_core`. A useful distilled core must be:

- Short enough to read above the fold.
- Specific enough to guide adaptation brainstorming.
- Free of raw evidence IDs.
- Focused on what to steal, what not to copy, and what to test next.
