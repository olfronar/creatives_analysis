# Submagic Product Research

## Positioning

Submagic is best treated as an AI short-form video production workspace, not only an AI caption generator. Its core business is SaaS for creators, agencies, marketers, media teams, coaches, and SMBs that need to turn raw or long-form video into platform-ready short-form content faster.

The working sales promise for adaptation brainstorming:

> Publish more high-retention short-form videos with less editing labor.

For creators and agencies, the strongest angles are speed, output volume, brand consistency, fewer manual edits, and the ability to turn one source video into multiple platform-ready variants.

## What To Sell

| Product area | Value for creators/agencies |
|---|---|
| AI captions | Faster captioning, animated styles, word emphasis, multilingual output, better mobile readability. |
| Magic Clips | Turns long-form videos into multiple short clips and helps surface hookable moments. |
| AI Auto Edit | Combines captions, emojis, keyword highlights, animations, contextual B-roll, zooms, silence removal, audio cleanup, and eye-contact correction. |
| B-roll and assets | Adds contextual visuals and media without forcing a full manual edit. |
| Brand Kit and templates | Helps agencies keep multiple client outputs consistent. |
| Publishing and scheduling | Supports the workflow after editing by preparing posts for major social platforms. |
| API and automation | Useful for high-volume teams and agencies with repeatable production systems. |

## Target Audience

Primary V1 audience: creators and agencies.

Subgroups:

- Solo creators who film more than they can edit.
- Coaches and educators turning talking-head content into short clips.
- Agencies producing recurring client Reels, TikToks, Shorts, and LinkedIn clips.
- Editors and content teams that need faster first drafts and brand-consistent outputs.

## Competitor Landscape

Use competitors as context, not as copy targets.

| Category | Examples | Submagic adaptation lens |
|---|---|---|
| Long-to-short clipping | OpusClip, Vizard, Klap, Munch, Pictory | Emphasize clip volume, hook discovery, and faster publishing loops. |
| Caption/social editors | Captions.ai, CapCut, Zubtitle, SendShort | Emphasize caption quality, templates, brand consistency, B-roll, and edit speed. |
| Broader editors | VEED, Kapwing, Descript, Adobe Premiere Pro | Emphasize simpler short-form workflow and fewer manual timeline decisions. |
| AI presenter/generation tools | HeyGen, Synthesia, InVideo | Emphasize repurposing and editing real footage rather than only generating from scratch. |

## Adaptation Angles

Preserve competitor creative mechanisms, then remap them to Submagic-specific proof:

- Before/after: raw talking-head clip to captioned, zoomed, B-roll-enhanced short.
- Time saved: manual edit stack to one Submagic workflow.
- Agency economics: more client variants without hiring another editor.
- Output volume: one webinar, podcast, or client call into many platform-ready clips.
- Brand control: reusable templates and brand kits across client accounts.
- Platform readiness: captions, framing, and publish-ready exports for TikTok, Reels, Shorts, LinkedIn, and Meta.

## Claim Discipline

Use official claims only when sourced and current. Treat self-reported numbers such as user counts, accuracy, time savings, and pricing as product-page claims that should be rechecked before use in paid ads.

For adaptation reports, prefer claims that can be shown in the video:

- show raw clip becoming polished output
- show captions syncing to speech
- show B-roll inserted from transcript context
- show multiple platform variants
- show brand kit/template reuse
