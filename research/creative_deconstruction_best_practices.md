# Creative Deconstruction Best Practices

## Scope

This note defines how to deconstruct short-form competitor video creatives for later Submagic adaptation. The target audience is creators and agencies, and the platform focus is multi-platform: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, Meta paid social, and LinkedIn.

The method is evidence-first. A reviewer must cite timestamps, transcript lines, OCR text, keyframes, scene notes, or audio observations before assigning a score.

## Core Method

Use timestamp bands rather than a single impression:

- `0-3s`: first-frame read, hook line, visual interrupt, product or problem visibility.
- `3-6s`: promise clarity and whether the viewer knows why to keep watching.
- `6-15s`: proof, demo, emotional turn, and pacing discipline.
- `midpoint`: retention risk, repeated shots, unclear transition, or proof delay.
- `CTA/end`: action clarity, product recall, and whether the CTA follows from the promise.

The most useful deconstruction output is not "this is good" or "this is bad." It is a reusable map: hook formula, promise, proof mechanism, edit rhythm, visual motif, CTA shape, platform fit, drop-off hypothesis, and safe Submagic adaptation path.

## Deconstruction Layers

Every deconstruction should separate four layers. Mixing these layers is the fastest way to create confident but low-quality analysis.

| Layer | Question | Example output |
|---|---|---|
| Observation | What is literally visible or audible? | `0.0-1.0s: creator face close-up; caption reads "Stop editing like this"; bass hit on first cut.` |
| Viewer perception | What might the viewer feel or understand at this moment? | `Immediate problem recognition for creators tired of repetitive editing.` |
| Strategic interpretation | What creative mechanism is operating? | `Anti-pattern hook + implied better workflow.` |
| Adaptation mechanism | What can be reused without copying expression? | `Open with a familiar editing mistake, then reveal Submagic as the workflow compression tool.` |

The report should never jump directly from observation to recommendation. It should first name the viewer perception and creative mechanism.

## Evidence Capture Checklist

Short-form ads are too fast for sparse summaries. Capture the evidence needed to reconstruct the actual viewing experience:

- First frame screenshot.
- Frames around `0.5s`, `1s`, `2s`, and `3s`.
- First spoken words and first caption text.
- First product, UI, logo, or brand appearance.
- Scene boundaries and cut density.
- OCR text with timestamp, position, and confidence where possible.
- Audio events: music start, beat drop, silence, voiceover start, sound effects, emphasis.
- Caption legibility: size, contrast, location, sync, safe-zone risk.
- Proof moments: before/after, demo, testimonial, metric, interface action, social proof.
- CTA moment: exact timestamp, wording, visual treatment, and whether audio reinforces it.

Uniform frame sampling is useful for inventory, but it is not enough for deconstruction. Hook analysis needs dense early sampling and event-based extraction.

## Mechanism Taxonomy

Extract the reusable mechanism, not the surface asset. The goal is to adapt at the level of persuasion structure while changing expression.

| Mechanism | What it does | Submagic adaptation path |
|---|---|---|
| Anti-pattern reveal | Shows the viewer a mistake they recognize. | "Stop manually fixing captions/pauses/B-roll one clip at a time." |
| Workflow compression | Collapses many tedious steps into one visible transformation. | Raw talking-head clip becomes captioned, cut, zoomed, and B-roll-enhanced. |
| Hidden labor exposed | Makes invisible effort feel painfully obvious. | Show the editor's timeline chaos before Submagic cleans the workflow. |
| Status reversal | Moves the user from amateur-looking output to professional-looking output. | Same raw footage, different perceived creator quality after Submagic edit. |
| Before/after relief | Creates tension through mess, then releases it through clarity. | Uncaptioned ramble becomes tight, readable, platform-ready short. |
| Expert taste transfer | Suggests the tool applies editing taste the user does not yet have. | Submagic chooses hooks, zooms, cuts, captions, and B-roll moments. |
| Volume unlock | Shifts from one finished video to many usable variants. | Long video becomes multiple platform-ready shorts for client or creator output. |
| Objection flip | Names a hesitation, then reverses it with proof. | "AI edits look generic" becomes branded captions/templates/proof scenes. |

If a mechanism cannot be named independently from the competitor's exact words, music, creator, or visual identity, it is not yet safe to adapt.

## Rubric Dimensions

Score each dimension from `0` to `3`.

| Dimension | What to inspect |
|---|---|
| Hook strength | Is the first frame or first line instantly legible? Does it create problem recognition, curiosity, surprise, status desire, or proof-first urgency? |
| Promise clarity | Can the viewer explain the payoff by second 3-6? One specific promise beats several vague claims. |
| Emotional arc | Does the ad move from pain, confusion, or desire toward relief, control, status, or speed? |
| Brand/product integration | Does the product appear early enough to be remembered while still fitting the story? |
| Visual memorability | Are there distinct faces, motion, interface moments, repeated symbols, contrast, or unusual shots? |
| Message hierarchy | Do spoken audio, captions, on-screen text, and visuals reinforce the same idea? |
| Pacing/edit rhythm | Does each second earn attention? Are cuts, captions, gestures, zooms, and audio changes timed for comprehension? |
| Core story/idea | Is there a clear transformation, contradiction, reveal, demo, proof, or before-after mechanism? |
| Strengths and weaknesses | Which creative mechanisms should be preserved, and which moments weaken retention or clarity? |
| CTA strength | Is the next action specific and matched to the promise? |
| Platform fit | Does the creative fit vertical mobile consumption, safe zones, caption behavior, sound behavior, and native platform expectations? |
| Likely drop-off points | Where might viewers leave because the promise is unclear, proof is delayed, shots repeat, captions overload, or the CTA feels disconnected? |
| Proof credibility | Is there believable evidence, demo, social proof, result, comparison, or mechanism? |
| Caption readability | Are captions readable on mobile, safely placed, and synchronized with speech? |
| Copycat/IP risk | Would adapting this too closely copy protected expression, brand identity, claims, music, or creator likeness? |
| Originality and relevance | Is the idea both different enough to be memorable and relevant enough to support the product promise? |
| Human truth | Does the creative understand a private frustration, desire, fear, or status tension in the audience? |
| Swappability | Could the brand/product be swapped with any other AI editor without changing the ad? If yes, the idea is too generic. |

## Platform Principles

Google's ABCD framework is a strong baseline: Attention, Branding, Connection, and Direction. For this workspace, translate that into: hook fast, integrate the product early, create audience relevance, and make the CTA direct.

TikTok's Creative Codes emphasize TikTok-first creation, trend/context awareness, production discipline, structure, sound, and refresh cadence. For deconstruction, this means judging whether the ad feels native to feed behavior rather than polished but interruptive.

Meta and Reels-style paid social should be judged by first-frame thumb-stop, fast product/problem visibility, mobile framing, and proof that can be understood while scrolling. LinkedIn variants can tolerate more explicit business framing, but they still need a clear opening promise.

## Creativity Research Lens

Advertising-creativity research repeatedly warns against treating "different" as automatically effective. The useful distinction is:

- originality or divergence: the ad feels novel, surprising, or distinctive
- appropriateness or relevance: the novelty still serves the audience, product, and context

A creative can fail by being relevant but invisible, or original but strategically irrelevant. For Submagic adaptation, good variants need both:

- fresh expression: a specific scene, tension, metaphor, proof format, or creator situation
- strategic fit: visible proof that Submagic reduces editing labor, improves output quality, increases variant volume, or protects brand consistency

This is why deconstruction should include both craft metrics and taste metrics. A clean hook and readable caption are not enough if the idea feels interchangeable with every other AI video editor.

## Viewer-State Timeline

For each timestamp band, record likely viewer state alongside evidence:

| Moment | Viewer question | Analysis target |
|---|---|---|
| `0-1s` | "What is this, and should I stop scrolling?" | First-frame clarity, face/gesture, visual interrupt, first caption, sound cue. |
| `1-3s` | "Is this about a problem or desire I recognize?" | Hook specificity, promise seed, audience fit. |
| `3-6s` | "What will I get if I keep watching?" | Promise clarity, product/problem visibility. |
| `6-15s` | "Do I believe this?" | Proof, demo, pacing, emotional movement. |
| midpoint | "Is this still paying off?" | Novel information, repetition, edit rhythm, proof delay. |
| CTA/end | "What should I do now?" | CTA specificity, product recall, next-step clarity. |

Drop-off points should be written as hypotheses about viewer state, not as performance facts.

## Research Signals

Recent multimodal ad-analysis research supports separating observable evidence from interpretation:

- `VideoAds` frames advertisement videos as temporally dense multimodal objects where summary, visual finding, and reasoning are separate tasks.
- `E-VAds` highlights commercial short videos as requiring product, scene, intent, and reasoning understanding rather than generic video captioning.
- Ad memorability research reinforces visual salience, story structure, and memorable cues, but it should not be treated as a direct predictor of conversion without campaign data.
- Story-video verbalization research suggests that converting temporal video into structured natural-language descriptions can improve zero-shot reasoning. For this workspace, that supports a dedicated "sensory/timeline verbalization" phase before scoring.
- Subjective-response and eye-tracking-oriented ad-video research suggests that viewer reaction is heterogeneous and multimodal. The system should record confidence and avoid assuming a single universal emotional response.
- Creative fatigue and ad discontinuation research reinforces that creative quality is not static. A good deconstruction should preserve angle diversity and testability, not only describe one "best" version.

## Failure Modes To Catch

- Scorekeeping without perception: assigning rubric scores before describing what the viewer sees, hears, and feels.
- Evidence theater: citing a timestamp that does not actually support the claim.
- Hook overfitting: treating the first line as the whole ad while ignoring proof and payoff.
- Platform flattening: assuming TikTok, Reels, Shorts, Meta, and LinkedIn reward the same expression.
- Feature-list adaptation: turning every idea into "captions + B-roll + save time" without a fresh proof scene.
- Copycat drift: preserving too much of the competitor's expression instead of the mechanism.
- Performance laundering: using words like "winning," "high-performing," or "conversion-driving" without campaign metrics.
- Generic SaaS language: claims that would still work if "Submagic" were replaced by another AI editor.

## Cautions

- Do not call a competitor creative "winning" without performance data.
- Drop-off points are hypotheses unless retention curves are provided.
- Platform best practices are correlational guidance, not guarantees.
- A strong hook can still fail if the body does not prove the promise.
- A competitor tactic should be adapted at the level of mechanism, not copied at the level of script, design, music, or brand identity.
- A source-cited platform heuristic is not a substitute for watching the ad carefully.
- Memorability, attention, engagement, and conversion are related but not interchangeable outcomes.
