# Creative Deconstruction Best Practices

## Scope

This note defines how to deconstruct short-form competitor video creatives for later Submagic adaptation. The target audience is creators and agencies, and the platform focus is multi-platform: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, Meta paid social, and LinkedIn.

The method is evidence-first. A reviewer must cite timestamps, transcript lines, OCR text, keyframes, scene notes, or audio observations before assigning a score.

## Core Method

Use timestamp bands rather than a single impression:

- `0-3s`: first-frame read, hook line, visual interrupt, product or problem visibility.
- `3-6s`: promise clarity and whether the viewer knows why to keep watching.
- `6-15s`: proof, demo, emotional turn, and pacing discipline.
- `midpoint`: retention risk, repeated shots, unclear transition, or proof delay.
- `CTA/end`: action clarity, product recall, and whether the CTA follows from the promise.

The most useful deconstruction output is not "this is good" or "this is bad." It is a reusable map: hook formula, promise, proof mechanism, edit rhythm, visual motif, CTA shape, platform fit, drop-off hypothesis, and safe Submagic adaptation path.

## Rubric Dimensions

Score each dimension from `0` to `3`.

| Dimension | What to inspect |
|---|---|
| Hook strength | Is the first frame or first line instantly legible? Does it create problem recognition, curiosity, surprise, status desire, or proof-first urgency? |
| Promise clarity | Can the viewer explain the payoff by second 3-6? One specific promise beats several vague claims. |
| Emotional arc | Does the ad move from pain, confusion, or desire toward relief, control, status, or speed? |
| Brand/product integration | Does the product appear early enough to be remembered while still fitting the story? |
| Visual memorability | Are there distinct faces, motion, interface moments, repeated symbols, contrast, or unusual shots? |
| Message hierarchy | Do spoken audio, captions, on-screen text, and visuals reinforce the same idea? |
| Pacing/edit rhythm | Does each second earn attention? Are cuts, captions, gestures, zooms, and audio changes timed for comprehension? |
| Core story/idea | Is there a clear transformation, contradiction, reveal, demo, proof, or before-after mechanism? |
| Strengths and weaknesses | Which creative mechanisms should be preserved, and which moments weaken retention or clarity? |
| CTA strength | Is the next action specific and matched to the promise? |
| Platform fit | Does the creative fit vertical mobile consumption, safe zones, caption behavior, sound behavior, and native platform expectations? |
| Likely drop-off points | Where might viewers leave because the promise is unclear, proof is delayed, shots repeat, captions overload, or the CTA feels disconnected? |
| Proof credibility | Is there believable evidence, demo, social proof, result, comparison, or mechanism? |
| Caption readability | Are captions readable on mobile, safely placed, and synchronized with speech? |
| Copycat/IP risk | Would adapting this too closely copy protected expression, brand identity, claims, music, or creator likeness? |

## Platform Principles

Google's ABCD framework is a strong baseline: Attention, Branding, Connection, and Direction. For this workspace, translate that into: hook fast, integrate the product early, create audience relevance, and make the CTA direct.

TikTok's Creative Codes emphasize TikTok-first creation, trend/context awareness, production discipline, structure, sound, and refresh cadence. For deconstruction, this means judging whether the ad feels native to feed behavior rather than polished but interruptive.

Meta and Reels-style paid social should be judged by first-frame thumb-stop, fast product/problem visibility, mobile framing, and proof that can be understood while scrolling. LinkedIn variants can tolerate more explicit business framing, but they still need a clear opening promise.

## Research Signals

Recent multimodal ad-analysis research supports separating observable evidence from interpretation:

- `VideoAds` frames advertisement videos as temporally dense multimodal objects where summary, visual finding, and reasoning are separate tasks.
- `E-VAds` highlights commercial short videos as requiring product, scene, intent, and reasoning understanding rather than generic video captioning.
- Ad memorability research reinforces visual salience, story structure, and memorable cues, but it should not be treated as a direct predictor of conversion without campaign data.

## Cautions

- Do not call a competitor creative "winning" without performance data.
- Drop-off points are hypotheses unless retention curves are provided.
- Platform best practices are correlational guidance, not guarantees.
- A strong hook can still fail if the body does not prove the promise.
- A competitor tactic should be adapted at the level of mechanism, not copied at the level of script, design, music, or brand identity.
